    \hypertarget{implementation}{%
\paragraph{Implementation}\label{implementation}}

Seven binary variables will be taken from the database. Each entry in
the database represents a single person in a crash. The variables used
are:

\textbf{K}illed - Whether or not the individual died in the crash.

\textbf{A}irbag - Whether or not the car has an airbag. (This dataset is
fairly old, before airbags were a requirement)

\textbf{B}elt - Whether or not the individual was wearing their
seatbelt.

\textbf{F}rontal - Whether or not the vehicle was hit in the front.

\textbf{S}ex - Gender of the individual.

\textbf{D}eploy - Whether or not the airbag was deployed. Only true for
cars with airbags.

\textbf{R}ole - Whether or the the individual was the driver or a
passenger.

    \hypertarget{conclusion}{%
\subsubsection{Conclusion}\label{conclusion}}

Overall, the implementation of a variety of inference methods on a
Bayesian Network was fairly straightforward once the model was defined.
Due to how the package is designed, creating such a model takes a
majority of the time, and analysis is fairly straightforward. For models
similar to the size of the one defined, exact inference is a fairly fast
method, and sampling takes a fair bit longer. However, for larger
models, sampling should be faster.
